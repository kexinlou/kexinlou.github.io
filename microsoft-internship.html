Microsoft Design Internship

Overview: Over the summer, I designed Microsoft 365 new users onboarding experience on Microsoft’s FUEL Growth & User Lifecycle team. The goal for my project is to help new users get the most out of their subscription.

Role: UX Design Intern

Tool Kits: Figma, Photoshop, UserTesting.com

Timeline: 12 week


00. Project Overview

In summer 2021, I interned at Microsoft’s FUEL Growth & User Lifecycle team. My project is about to design an onboarding experience for Microsotf 365 new users. On a high level, I worked on defining the problem statement, doing user research, ideating design solutions, conducting usability testing, and collaborated with the cross-functional team with PM and researchers on product strategy. While I can’t disclose the details of my project due to NDA, feel free to contact me if you would like to learn more!


01. Key Takeaway

This is definitely a memorable summer. I learned, grew, and absorbed as much as I can from this experience. Despite the internship was virtual, I still got chances to meet amazing designers and made connections with them. Here are some biggest takeaways from my internship.

Build work on others

Working in a big tech company like Microsoft, I was surrounded by talented people. It’s extremely important to build my work on others’ work, listen to their feedback and always seek out for different perspectives. During my internship, I proposed the problem statement and hypothesis based on past researches and also ideated the design solution on others’ onboarding work.

Over-communicate

Remote work could be challenging, especailly with cross-functional partners. Always over-communicate to bring clarity and learn from each other. I collaborated with a senior PM and also synced with researchers for feedback and updates throughout the internship. Being a good designer doesn’t just mean to be pixel perfect but also how to comunicate my ideas and thoughts clearly and explain the rationale behind each design decision.

Explore, immerse, and learn

Microsoft has its own design system Fluent. While it’s handy to take components and design patterns from the existing design system, I tried not to limit myself and go wild at the beginning. Exploring other designs is a great way to improve my visual design skills and also think out of box. Other than design skills, Microsoft has so many exciting opportunities. I also participated in an intern co-creation project and connected with other interns and designers.
